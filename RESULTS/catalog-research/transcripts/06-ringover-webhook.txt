This is a walkthrough of a project called MyWebhookListener, which integrates Ringover webhook data with our Google Sheet for campaigns. This project that I've completed for a client that takes webhook information from, in this case Ringover, and processes that data through various filters that then allows us to classify various Google Sheets filled with campaign information with the proper tagging information that's derived from Ringover. So when a call center performs calls, those calls are received through the webhook and then processed through for whether or not it's a brand new month, in which case it creates a brand new Google Sheet, or if it's within the existing month, it goes through and Ringover performs two types of webhooks. One for the initial call, which is logged here, and then one for the subsequent call where all the tagging information is, which is then logged here into the same Google Sheet and we keep a running total back into internal databases. This can be easily adapted for any type of call center type webhook information as well as any endpoint database on the other side. If you have any questions, please let me know.
